# Structured Data and AI Discoverability

> Structured Data | 3 min read | Published 2026-03-18

Structured data gives AI engines explicit context about your page. Instead of guessing what your documentation covers, they can read machine-readable metadata that says exactly what it is.

## The Key Formats

Three structured data formats matter most for documentation:

- JSON-LD: Schema.org markup embedded in your page. Tells search engines your page is a "TechArticle" or "APIReference" with specific properties.
- OpenGraph: Meta tags that define how your page appears when shared. AI engines also use these for context.
- Breadcrumbs: Navigation paths that show where your page sits in the documentation hierarchy.

## Canonical URLs

Canonical URLs tell bots which version of a page is the "real" one. Without them, AI engines might index duplicate versions of the same content and dilute your page's authority.

This is especially important for documentation sites that serve content at multiple URLs (versioned docs, locale variants, etc.).
